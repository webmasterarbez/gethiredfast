## 1. The 2025 Entry-Level Job Market

### Market Conditions: Challenging

The Class of 2025 faces one of the toughest job markets in a decade:

| Metric | 2025 Data | Source |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Hiring decline | Down 16% vs 2024, 44% below 2022 | [SHRM](https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/news/talent-acquisition/class-of-2025-college-grads-face-uncertain-job-market) |
| Unemployment rate | 5.3% for recent grads | [NY Fed](https://www.newyorkfed.org/research/college-labor-market) |
| Underemployment rate | 41.8% (highest since 2020) | [NY Fed](https://www.newyorkfed.org/research/college-labor-market) |
| Applications per student | 10 average (up from 6 in 2024) | [NACE](https://www.naceweb.org/about-us/press/class-of-2025-college-grads-responded-to-challenges-of-a-tough-job-market) |
| Job search start | 6.4 months before graduation | [NACE](https://www.naceweb.org/about-us/press/class-of-2025-college-grads-responded-to-challenges-of-a-tough-job-market) |

### Key Insight: The College Degree Advantage is Narrowing

> "For the first time in modern history, a bachelor's degree is no longer a reliable path to professional employment." — Gad Levanon, Chief Economist, Burning Glass Institute

- Job-finding rates for college grads have declined to match high school graduate levels
- AI is automating entry-level positions traditionally filled by graduates
- Companies are adopting skills-based hiring over credential-based hiring

### Growing Industries
- Healthcare (aging population driving demand)
- Engineering
- Technology (despite layoffs, still strong)

### What This Means for Community College Students
- **Associate degrees + certifications may offer faster entry** to growing healthcare/tech fields
- **Skills matter more than ever** — focus on demonstrable abilities
- **Internships/experience critical**: 84% of Class of 2025 did experiential learning
- **Salary premium for internships**: $59,059 average vs $44,048 without

## 3. AI Video Interviews (HireVue & Similar)

### The New Reality

**70% of companies plan to use AI for hiring by 2025**, with HireVue leading the transformation.

### What HireVue Analyzes

| Factor | How It's Measured |
|--------|-------------------|
| Communication | Clarity, conciseness, language quality |
| Non-verbal cues | Facial expressions, eye contact, gestures |
| Voice | Pace, intonation, modulation |
| Body language | Posture, hand movements |
| Content | Job-relevant skills and experience |

### Format
- 3-5 pre-recorded questions
- 30 seconds to think per question
- Up to 3 minutes to answer (aim for 90 seconds)
- One-way recording (no interviewer present)

### Key Success Factors

1. **Technical Setup**
   - Neutral background
   - Camera at eye level
   - Key light at 45° angle, above eye level
   - Hardwire internet if possible

2. **Answer Structure**
   - Use STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) or SOAR
   - Keep answers under 90 seconds
   - Practice with built-in practice questions

3. **Professional Presentation**
   - Dress as for in-person interview
   - Research company beforehand
   - Maintain eye contact with camera (not screen)

### Important Warning
> "Beyond practicing like you would for any other type of interview, there's no course you can take that will help you game an AI-driven job assessment from HireVue."

## 4. First-Generation Student Challenges

### The Scope
- 35% of first-gen students have had NO experience with career services
- 48% believe colleges should prioritize helping with internships/jobs

### Key Barriers

| Barrier | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Limited Networks** | 70% of jobs come through networking; 80% never advertised |
| **Lack of Mentors** | No family guidance on professional norms |
| **Imposter Syndrome** | Feel like they don't belong, undermines confidence |
| **Limited Career Awareness** | Often only know traditional paths (doctor, lawyer, engineer) |
| **Financial Pressure** | More likely to carry student debt, creating stress |

### Successful Institutional Programs

1. **CSU Fullerton "I Am First"**
   - Personalized mentorship
   - Priority microinternship placement
   - $500 + $500 stipends

2. **Grinnell College**
   - Assigns career advisor to all first-years
   - Contact begins summer before freshman year

3. **Hamilton College "First Year Forward"**
   - Year-long bridge program
   - Monthly professional development sessions
   - 3 informational interviews required
   - Stipend for summer experience

## 8. Networking for Introverts

### Introvert Advantages

| Strength | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| Enhanced listening | 37% better understanding of others' needs |
| Depth over breadth | Higher quality relationships |
| Thoughtful engagement | More meaningful conversations |

### Effective Strategies

1. **Start small** — Don't try multiple events at once
2. **Choose smaller gatherings** — 42% more valuable connections
3. **Online platforms first** — LinkedIn, forums (49M use LinkedIn for job search weekly)
4. **Content-based networking** — Share articles, create content (29% more inbound requests)
5. **Give first mindset** — Share resources, make intros (26% more positive responses)
6. **Pre-event research** — Identify 3-5 target contacts (31% higher conversation quality)
7. **Activity-based networking** — Volunteer, join committees (38% less social anxiety)
8. **One-on-one follow-up** — Build depth (44% higher relationship value)

## 9. Resume Writing with No Experience

### Key Strategies

1. **Use functional or hybrid format** — Skills before experience
2. **Strong resume objective** — Goals + relevant skills
3. **Expand education section** — GPA (if 3.5+), honors, awards, committees
4. **Include extracurriculars** — Shows motivation and teamwork
5. **Non-traditional experience counts** — Tutoring, babysitting, family business
6. **Focus on skills** — 5-10 relevant hard and soft skills
7. **Keep to one page** — Standard for entry-level
8. **Tailor every application** — Customize for each job

### Mindset Shift
> "When you shift your mindset and think of your role as a student as a job, you'll find that you actually have a great deal of experience that you can offer to a potential employer."
